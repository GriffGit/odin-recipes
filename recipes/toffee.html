Homemade Toffee Bits

This is a simple recipe to make toffee from scratch. It is a lot easier than you think!

Prep Time:5 min     Cook Time: 10-15 min    Total Time: 25min

Equipment

  • Heavy Bottomed Small Saucepan or Pot
  • Whisk
  • Candy Thermometer
  • Parchement Paper
  • Baking Sheet
  • Parchment Paper

Ingredients

  • 1 stick (113g) unstalted American butter
    • Do not use European-style butter in this recipe. European-style butter containes 82% or more butterfat and will cause the toffee to separate.
  • 1 cup (200g) light brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions

  1. Line a small rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper
  2. In a heavy bottomed small sauce pan or pot melt the butter over medium heat. Add the sugar and salt and whisk until combined. Cook, continuously stirring (do not stop mixing), until the mixture looks like melted peanut butter and a candy thermometer reaches 295 to 305 degrees F, about 10 to 15 minutes.
  3. If the mixture separates at all, remove from heat and whisk vigourously until recombined
  4. Immediately and carefully pour the hot toffee onto the prepared baking pan, allowing it to spread into an even layer. Let cool and harden for about 20 minnutes.
  5. Place the sheet of toffe on a cutting board or in a zip top bag. Use a mallet, rolling pin, or heavy object to crack it into small pieces. Store in air tight container for up to 1 week.
